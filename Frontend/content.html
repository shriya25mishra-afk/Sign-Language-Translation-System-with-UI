SignLingo

Real-Time Sign Language Translator

The Real-Time Sign Language Translator is an innovative system designed to bridge the communication gap between hearing-impaired individuals and non-signers. Using advanced machine learning and computer vision techniques, the translator detects and recognizes hand gestures captured via a webcam or smartphone camera, and converts them instantly into text or speech output.

The system employs a deep learning model trained on various sign language datasets. It processes the input video frames, extracts hand landmarks, and identifies gestures using a neural network. The recognized gestures are then translated into meaningful words or phrases, making it possible for real-time communication.

This project contributes to accessibility and inclusivity by helping people with hearing and speech disabilities communicate effortlessly in real-world scenarios like education, healthcare, and public services.

“Breaking the silence with technology — empowering communication for everyone.”
